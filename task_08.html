You are an investment-committee analyst supporting Tianlong Electronics' Strategy Office. The board is meeting in 14 days to decide whether to approve Tianlong's India smartphone market entry. Tianlong Electronics is a mid-sized Chinese consumer-electronics OEM. The Strategy Office has assembled a research dossier drawing on: (a) anonymized synthesis of nine prior Chinese-OEM India entries 2014–2022 (Entrants A–I) from File-India_PriorEntrants_ResearchBrief.pdf, (b) current India smartphone market data from File-India_Smartphone_MarketData_v3.xlsx, (c) Tianlong's pre-decision strategy deck from File- Tianlong_StrategyDeck_Internal.pptx, and (d) competitive intelligence on those prior entrants from File- Competitor_ChineseEntries_India.csv. The capex envelope is USD 80M across years 0 - 2, the post-tax project IRR hurdle is 18% over a 5-year horizon. Using ONLY the four attached files, produce a 5-section Board Memo recommending GO, NO-GO, or CONDITIONAL GO on Tianlong's India market entry. Quantify the recommendation: addressable TAM (units and USD), SAM definition and sizing, Year-5 SOM revenue, 5-year cumulative gross profit, project IRR, and decision against the 18% hurdle. Structure your response as a Board Memo with these five sections in this order: 1.Recommendation (one line: GO, NO-GO, or CONDITIONAL GO) 2.Market sizing (TAM, SAM, Year-5 SOM in units and USD) 3.Financial case (5-year revenue ramp, gross profit, project IRR vs hurdle) 4.Strategic design (architecture, channel, geography, brand) 5.Key risks and conditions Note- •No external search. Use only the files provided. •Cite the file and tab/page/slide for any quantitative claim. •State assumptions explicitly. If a figure is uncertain, give a range. •Do not exceed 800 words across all five sections combined.

Step 1 - Identify and quantify the buried methodology adjustment
File- India_Smartphone_MarketData_v3.xlsx, Tab 12 (Methodology_Notes), Note 4, discloses that the 140M sub-Rs 25K headline figure on Tab 8 includes:
•8M units smart-feature-phones (KaiOS / JioPhone class); not addressable for Tianlong's mid-tier consumer SKUs
• 12M units B2B / enterprise / government bulk procurement - not addressable through Tianlong's planned consumer-direct channel motion
• 4M units refurbished / secondary resale - not net-new addressable demand 
Total non-addressable: 24M units (~17% of the headline 140M).
Adjusted addressable TAM: 140 − 24 = 116M units.

Step 2 - Convert TAM to USD using the FX assumption
Using the weighted ASP of Rs 14,500 (Tab 7) and the FY26 reference rate Rs 87.5/USD (Tab 2):
• Headline TAM (USD): 140M × Rs 14,500 / 87.5 = USD 23.2B
• Adjusted TAM (USD): 116M × Rs 14,500 / 87.5 = USD 19.2B

Step 3 - Apply SAM definition (File- Tianlong_StrategyDeck_Internal.pptx, Slide 14)
Phase 1 SAM = Tier-1 cities + Top-50 Tier-2 cities = ~45% of national handset demand:
• Adjusted SAM (units): 116M × 45% = 52.2M units
• Adjusted SAM (USD): USD 19.2B × 45% = USD 8.65B

Step 4 - Apply Year-5 share assumption to compute SOM
Yr5 share assumption: 4% of SAM (per File- Tianlong_StrategyDeck_Internal.pptx Slide 11; consistent with Entrant G's Year-5 outcome of 4.2% in File- Competitor_ChineseEntries_India.csv):
• Yr5 SOM (units): 52.2M × 4% = 2.088M units
• Yr5 SOM (USD): 2.088M × Rs 14,500 / 87.5 = USD 346M

Step 6 - Compute IRR against the 18% hurdle
Capex profile (File- Tianlong_StrategyDeck_Internal.pptx Slide 9): Yr0 = $35M, Yr1 = $25M, Yr2 = $20M (total $80M). Treating gross profit as a proxy for incremental project cash flow and assuming a Yr5 terminal multiple of 4× Yr5 GP, the resulting project IRR on the adjusted-TAM base case is approximately 16.5–17.5%.
This fails the 18% hurdle by 50–150 bps.

Headline-basis (incorrect) IRR: ~22.8% - would clear hurdle and recommend GO.
