The Terminal General Manager needs a recommendation within one week. A wrong intervention will either increase operating cost without improving berth productivity or misallocate crane and labor resources across vessel types, worsening overall OEE. A structured operations document with the following sections in this exact order: 1. OEE Breakdown Table - Availability, Performance, and Quality rates for each vessel type, with OEE calculated to two decimal places 2. Loss Waterfall - Ranked loss contributors by vessel type in descending order of throughput impact 3. Multi-Product Line Sequencing Decision - Which vessel type to prioritize first and why, using OEE and throughput logic 4. Monthly Financial Impact Table - Penalty cost, lost throughput contribution, and total monthly exposure for each vessel type 5. Ranked Intervention Plan - Exactly two interventions, one per vessel type, ranked by financial impact, with decision commit Do not present prose only. Every section must follow the exact structure listed above.

That means the FMCG performance loss is structurally linked to the shared operator pool rather than to yard, gate, or customs factors.
 7. The contract notes set the chemical vessel gross crane-rate benchmark at 22 moves per hour. The intended actual rate used in the worked answer is 20.1 moves per hour, so chemical performance is 20.1 / 22 = 91.4 percent.

 10. The quality log states the chemical vessel’s re-handle rate is 9.2 percent and that 71 percent of these re-handles are due to container sequencing errors. Therefore the dominant quality loss driver for the chemical line is sequencing failure, not mechanical downtime.
 11. FMCG OEE is therefore 91.6 × 83.6 × 96.3 = 73.8
 12. Chemical OEE is 96.0 × 91.4 × 90.8 = 79.7
