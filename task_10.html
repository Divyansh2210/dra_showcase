You are part of the Service Operations Excellence team at a digital lending NBFC responsible for optimizing field collections performance. The field collections function recovers delinquent loans through on-ground agents deployed across urban and Tier 3/4 geographies. Over the last two quarters, the COO has aggressively scaled field operations: - Field agent headcount increased by 50% - Serviceable geographic coverage expanded by 60% (new Tier 3/4 regions) - Total field visits increased by ~70% Internal dashboards interpret this increase in activity and coverage as improved productivity. However, headline activity metrics (total visits, visits per agent) include repeat visits and systematically overstate effective customer coverage and productive effort. Despite this expansion: - Total recovery ₹ increased by <2% - Recovery per agent declined by ~35% - Cost per recovery increased significantly - Average time per successful recovery increased This creates a structural contradiction where increased operational effort does not translate into proportional recovery outcomes. The COO must present a clear, quantified diagnosis within 5 days. A misdiagnosis risks: - Continued cost escalation (~₹20-25 Cr annually) - Inefficient capital allocation toward field expansion - Deterioration in portfolio quality due to delayed recoveries Task (Precise) Using ONLY the provided datasets (agent activity logs, recovery outcomes, geo-operational data, and case allocation files): 1. Diagnose the root causes of declining field collection efficiency 2. Quantify impact across: - Agent productivity (true output per unit effort) - Geographic efficiency (travel vs productive utilization) - Case allocation effectiveness 3. Identify and rank the top 3 drivers by quantified impact on recovery ₹, ensuring that one primary driver clearly dominates 4. Support each driver using metrics derived from granular data (not reported summaries) 5. Recommend targeted interventions for each driver without altering headcount or expanding geographic coverage Important Constraints - Do NOT use external data - Base all conclusions strictly on provided datasets - The datasets contain substantial noise (≥60% of records), including: - repeat visits - inactive or non-recoverable cases - legacy allocations - redundant or low-quality activity - Many of these records inflate activity metrics without contributing to recovery outcomes - Reported productivity metrics may be directionally misleading - True performance must be reconstructed from granular data - Exactly one primary driver must emerge as dominant - Answers that do not identify a single dominant driver with quantified superiority will be considered incorrect Output Format (STRICT) 1. Executive Summary (3-4 bullet points) 2. Root Cause Diagnosis (structured issue tree + quantified impact) 3. Top 3 Drivers (ranked by ₹ impact, with supporting metrics) 4. Targeted Interventions (linked to each driver and constraints) 5. Final Recommendation (1 sentence, decisive) Decision Commit Provide a clear and final prioritization of drivers, explicitly identifying: - The single dominant driver - The ranked order of the top 3 drivers by impact on recovery amount
Lazy AI Test (Must Fail)

A standard model will rely on headline activity metrics (total visits, visits per agent) as indicators of productivity and fail to normalize for repeat visits and travel time.

Failure occurs in the following sequence:

1. Activity Misinterpretation
   - Treats total visits as effective coverage  
   - Does not compute unique customer coverage or repeat visit rate  

2. Effort Misallocation
   - Assumes all working time is productive  
   - Does not separate travel time from recovery effort  

3. Output Misattribution
   - Attributes decline in recovery per agent to agent performance or customer quality  
   - Does not compare recovery per visit vs recovery per unique customer  

4. Allocation Blind Spot
   - Accepts priority scores as valid without testing correlation with recovery outcomes  
   - Fails to detect misallocation of high-value cases  

Error Propagation:
- Overestimates effective coverage by ≥15-20%  
- Underestimates non-productive effort by ≥30-40%  
- Misattributes ≥60% of the ₹18-25 Cr inefficiency  

Incorrect Outcome:
- Identifies agent productivity or market conditions as primary issue  
- Recommends training, monitoring, or further scaling  
- Fails to identify allocation inefficiency as dominant driver  

Expert Test (Must Pass)

A domain expert will:

1. Normalize activity into unique customer coverage  
2. Quantify repeat visit inflation and isolate non-incremental effort  
3. Decompose working time into travel vs productive time  
4. Evaluate allocation effectiveness by linking priority scores to recovery outcomes  
5. Compare recovery efficiency across effort-normalized metrics  
6. Attribute impact across drivers and rank them by ₹ contribution  

Correct Outcome:
- Case allocation inefficiency identified as dominant driver (≥40%)  
- Geographic inefficiency and duplicate effort identified as secondary drivers  
- Driver contributions sum to ~100%  
- Conclusion focuses on optimizing allocation and deployment of effort  

Determinism Validation

Given:
- Consistent repeat visit inflation  
- Measurable travel inefficiency  
- Weak allocation-performance linkage  

All valid analytical paths converge to the same result:
- One dominant driver (allocation inefficiency)  
- Two secondary drivers (geographic inefficiency, duplicate effort)  

No alternative combination of drivers satisfies:
- Metric thresholds  
- Impact attribution  
- Dominance condition  

Why This Task Matters

This task evaluates whether the model can:

- Distinguish activity vs effective output in field operations  
- Detect hidden inefficiencies masked by scale expansion  
- Identify misallocation of operational effort despite increased capacity  
- Perform multi-source, noise-heavy operational diagnosis under constraints
Decision Archetype: Ranking of Alternatives  

Step 1: Normalize Activity into Effective Coverage
Source: Agent_Activity_Log.csv  
Compute at aggregate level:
- Total_Visits = SUM(visits)  
- Unique_Customers = SUM(unique_customers)  
- Repeat_Visits = SUM(repeat_visits)  
Derived:
- Repeat_Visit_Rate = Repeat_Visits / Total_Visits  
- Coverage_Efficiency = Unique_Customers / Total_Visits  
Validation:
- Assess whether a material gap exists between Total_Visits and Unique_Customers  
Inference:
If repeat visits form a significant share of total visits, headline activity metrics overstate effective coverage.

Step 2: Normalize Recovery Output per Unit Effort
Source: Agent_Activity_Log.csv + Recovery_Data.csv  
Join:
- Map case_id from Agent_Activity_Log to Recovery_Data to link visits with recovery outcomes  
Compute:
- Total_Recovery = SUM(recovery_amount)  
- Recovery_per_Agent = Total_Recovery / number_of_agents  
- Recovery_per_Visit = Total_Recovery / Total_Visits  
- Recovery_per_Unique_Customer = Total_Recovery / Unique_Customers  
Validation:
- Compare relative efficiency across:
  - Recovery_per_Visit vs Recovery_per_Unique_Customer  
  - Observed recovery vs expected proportional output based on activity
  Expected_Recovery = Total_Visits × (Recovery_per_Unique_Customer)
Inference:
If recovery per visit is materially lower than recovery per unique customer, efficiency loss is driven by redundant effort rather than customer-side factors.

This establishes the contradiction: activity increases while effective output per effort is inefficient.

Step 3: Quantify Geographic Utilization Loss
Source: Geo_Operational_Data.csv  
Compute:
- Travel_Time_Share = travel_time_min / (travel_time_min + productive_time_min)  
Segment:
- Compare Travel_Time_Share across region_type (Urban vs Rural)  
- Compute productivity per agent by region_type  
Validation:
- Assess whether rural regions exhibit:
  - Higher travel time share  
  - Lower productive output per unit time  
Inference:
Higher travel burden reduces effective utilization of field capacity.

Step 4: Evaluate Case Allocation Effectiveness
Source: Case_Allocation.csv + Recovery_Data.csv  
Join:
- Map case_id across allocation and recovery datasets  
Compute:
- Correlation between priority_score and recovered_flag  
- Average loan_value for:
  - assigned cases  
  - recovered cases  
- Reassignment_Rate = SUM(reassigned_flag) / total_cases  
Validation:
- Assess whether:
  - priority_score aligns with recovery outcomes  
  - higher loan_value cases are proportionately recovered  
  - reassignment frequency is material  
Inference:
Weak alignment indicates inefficient allocation of field effort to recovery opportunities.

Step 5: Estimate Non-Productive Effort Share
Combine outputs from Steps 1 and 3:
Compute:
- Non_Productive_Visit_Share = Repeat_Visits / Total_Visits  
- Non_Productive_Time_Share = Travel_Time_Share  
Derived:
- Total_Non_Productive_Effort approximated as:
Repeat_Visit_Share + Travel_Time_Share (directional assessment, not additive) 
Validation:
- Assess whether a substantial portion of total effort is non-productive  
Inference:
A large share of operational effort does not translate into recovery output.

Step 6: Attribute Impact Across Drivers (Derived, Not Assumed)
Estimate contribution of each driver by isolating its effect on recovery inefficiency:
- Case Allocation Inefficiency
  - Estimate loss using difference between:
    - Expected recovery based on loan_value distribution  
    - Actual recovery outcomes  
  - Captures impact of misprioritized and under-allocated high-value cases  
- Geographic Inefficiency
  - Estimate loss using productivity reduction attributable to:
    - Higher travel_time share  
    - Lower productive output in high-travel regions  
- Duplicate / Low-Quality Visits
  - Estimate loss using:
    - Repeat visit share  
    - Incremental effort that does not generate proportional recovery  
Normalize contributions:
- Total impact across drivers = 100%  

Rank drivers based on relative contribution.

Step 7: Reconcile with Total Business Impact
Aggregate:
- Estimated inefficiency across all drivers  
Validate:
- Total estimated impact aligns with observed business impact range  
Inference:
Drivers collectively explain observed decline in recovery efficiency.

Final Synthesis
Decline in field collection efficiency is driven by inefficient allocation and deployment of field effort, rather than insufficient capacity.
